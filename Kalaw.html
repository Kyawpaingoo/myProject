Kalaw

The town was popular with the British during colonial rule. Kalaw is the main setting of the novel Das Herzenh√∂ren by Jan-Philipp Sendker.

The hill station is located at an elevation of 1310 metres, 50 km from the Inle lake. Kalaw is famous for hiking and trekking.[1] Many trekking trails ranging from nearby places to Inle Lake and Pindaya.[2] The town still feels like a high-altitude holiday resort-the air is cool, the atmosphere is calm and the tree-lined streets still contain a smattering of colonial-era architecture-while the surrounding hills are fine for relatively easy day or overnight treks to Danu, Danaw, Palaung, Pa-O and Taung Yo villages.[3]

How to Get There

Flights arrive at Heho airport, 26km south east of Kalaw. The journey from Heho to Kalaw takes around one hour by taxi and costs K30,000; you may be able to save money by booking your taxi in advance through your hotel.

Cheaper options are pick-up trucks or buses, but these are far less convenient - and must be picked up from the town of Heho, which is three kilometres south east of the airport.

Kalaw-Inle Trek

Kalaw-Inle Trek is one of the most popular routes for hiking between the two towns for tourists who love nature. The rich natural beauty surrounding these towns comprising of the mountains and the lake make it a top attraction. The trek usually lasts for three days, but you can extend or have a short trek depending on your preference.

Aung Chan Tha Pagoda

Aung Chan Tha pagoda is an ancient Buddhist temple located in the city of Kalaw in Myanmar. It is a beautiful pagoda famous for the golden and silver mosaic tiles adorning the roof, which glows during the sunset and sunrise, drawing many tourists to Aung Chan Tha.

Kalaw-Myin Ma Hti Village

This morning trek with your local guide leads you to the spectacular Myin Ma Hti Cave, home to a very special collection of gilded Buddhist statues. Other treasures contained within the cave's chambers include an ancient pagoda: supposedly built more than 2,000 years ago. Close by, the tribal village of Myin Ma offers visitors a fascinating insight into rural Burmese life. Choose from either a further 3-4 hour hike back to Kalaw or a vehicle transfer.
